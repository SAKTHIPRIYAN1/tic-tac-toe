TIC TAC TOE

About

  • Tic Tac Toe is a classic two-player game that requires strategic thinking and planning to outwit your opponent.
  • The game can be played using just a simple grid and X and O markers, making it easily accessible and playable anywhere.
  • An early variation of tic-tac-toe was played in the Roman Empire, around the first century BC. It was called terni lapilli (three pebbles at a time) and instead of having any number of pieces, each player had only three; thus, they had to move them around to empty spaces to keep playing
  • Due to its straightforward nature and limited number of moves, Tic Tac Toe games are usually quick to play, making it an ideal choice for a short and engaging activity.

Rules

  • Tic Tac Toe is played on a 3x3 grid, providing 9 cells for gameplay.
  • Two players participate, one using "X" and the other using "O," taking turns to mark empty cells.
  • The goal is to form a horizontal, vertical, or diagonal line of three of your own markers.
  • Players alternate turns, placing their marker in an empty cell during each turn until a player wins or the grid is filled, resulting in a draw.
